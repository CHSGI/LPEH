Nigerian Energy Ambassadors

REPORT ON THE NIGERIAN ENERGY AMBASSADORS PROJECT HELD AT AMERICAN SPACECcHUB ON 19TH AND 20TH SEPTEMBER 2017.
TEAM: Azizat Olusola Gbadegesin (TechWomen 2016)
Oluremi Hamid (TechWomen 2016) Chimaobi Nna (La-Peonular Energy Hub Ltd)

BRIEF:
INTRODUCTION:
The U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs, Alumni TIES and World Learning provided a grant following an Alumni TIES on Access to Energy. The funds provided were to execute the Nigerian Energy Ambassadors Project. This project, a La-Peonular Energy Hub Ltd initiative, now in its second year, was set up to raise a generation of energy-conscious youth who would play an important role in improving access to energy in Nigeria. The target group was for students in Primary, Secondary and Tertiary institutions in Lagos State, Nigeria. The third component on Energypreneruship held at the CcHub, Sabo, Yaba Lagos on the 19th and 20th September 2017.

Energypreneurship:

Forty-five (45) students of University of Lagos and Yaba College of Technology were at CcHub, Yaba Lagos, for sessions on Energypreneurship. As Nigerian graduates go for a compulsory 1-year National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) Program where they are posted to different states of the country, these set of participants would be the driving-force for pushing energy businesses in the different geopolitical regions of the country.

On the first day, Mr. Segun Adaju, the President of the Renewable Energy Association of Nigeria (REAN) honoured our invitation and engaged the participants for over an hour on RE business prospects in Nigeria. He encouraged the participants to believe in themselves and aspire for greatness. Olakunle Owoeye (MWF 2016 Nigeria) also shared with them his experiences in promoting the solar-kiosk business in Nigeria, by encouraging small business-owners to stop the use of small petrol generators (commonly referred to as ‘I-beta-pass-my-neighbour’ in local parlance) and adopt the use of solar PV systems. This session also included some technical lessons including Load profile calculations, System sizing and Fabrication of solar kiosks. The following session was Oluremi Hamid’s (TechWomen 2016)’s training on Business Ethics they need to imbibe to be successful in their energy businesses. She also spoke about Residential and Commercial Power Solutions, Target market analysis and providing customer-specific solutions, how to grow your customer database and the basics of writing proposals to prospective customers. After the training sessions, participants were split into three (3) groups and started work on developing business plans for different energy-related businesses.

Day 2 started in earnest with Prosper Amuquandoh’s (MWF 2016, Ghana) presentations on Focusing on Success and setting SMART goals. He shared his skills and experience in working with a government regulatory agency in Ghana and as the CEO of The Smart and Green Energy Group. He also spoke on his invention – the Energy Cooking pot, and how this has opened opportunities for him. Chimaobi shared insights on Career paths in Industrial Energy management and opportunities in the RE and EE Industry. After the facilitators sessions were over, participants broke up into the groups formed the previous day to continue work on their business plans. Thereafter, each group was represented by a team-member who presented their proposals to the entire class.


Outputs:
After the sessions, a WhatsApp group of all participants and the Project team was formed. The participants are to continue working on their group projects after their school examinations. The Project Team has also been providing support with information for funding projects, trainings and other opportunities that would be useful to the continuous growth and development of the Energy Ambassadors.

APPRECIATION:
We are grateful to the staff of the U.S. Consulate, Lagos and CcHub for making the venue available and conducive for the entire duration of our training sessions.

Secondary Institutions

– Waste-to-Energy and Careers Day

Waste handling and management has been a daunting task in a country with a population of over 180 million Nigeria. This year, 90 students from International School, Akoka, Lagos were taught about identifying opportunities in waste and its conversion to useful energy. They also met five professionals in the energy sector on the Career day and had an insight into working in the energy sector. Participants were selected form JS1- SS2 and would be the pioneers of the Energy Ambassadors Club at the International School, Lagos.


Primary Institutions

– Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency

Sequel to the Energy Summer School in 2016, this year 40 pupils who attended the Summer classes at University of Lagos Staff School were trained on Renewable Energy and Energy efficiency. Activities they participated in include word search puzzles, cartoons, quiz competitions and learning about different lighting types and energy conservation in their homes and schools. At the end, they were presented with their certificates of participation which they proudly displayed as the new Energy ambassadors of their school.
